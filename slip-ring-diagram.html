How a Slip Ring Works Inside a Cord Reel

The component that transfers power from a fixed mount to a spinning spool

Cross-Section View
Why this matters for your 240V project:

On a 120V reel, the three conductors are Hot, Neutral, and Ground. On your 240V/20A circuit (NEMA 6-20), those same three conductors become Hot 1 (L1), Hot 2 (L2), and Ground — no neutral needed. The slip ring doesn't know or care what voltage is passing through it. What matters is:

1. Insulation rating — the gap between the rings must handle 240V without arcing. Consumer reels are typically insulated for 300V+, so this is usually fine.

2. Current rating — the brushes and rings must handle 20A continuous. A reel already rated for 20A at 120V handles the same 20A at 240V.

3. Contact quality — worn brushes create resistance, which creates heat. This is true at any voltage but gets worse under higher loads. Inspect brushes periodically.

⚠ The slip ring is the only electrical component in the reel that matters for your voltage question. The spring, spool, housing, and bracket are purely mechanical.
