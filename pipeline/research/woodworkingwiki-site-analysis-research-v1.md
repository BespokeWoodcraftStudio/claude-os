## 3. Update Frequency Analysis

### Publishing Timeline

- **Phase 1 (Feb–Aug 2015):** 9 posts published — initial burst of content
- **Gap:** ~22 months of silence (Aug 2015 → Jun 2017)
- **Phase 2 (Jun 2017):** 9 posts published or updated in a single day (Jun 18, 2017) — likely a bulk update/republish event
- **Phase 3 (Feb–Apr 2018):** 4 posts published — final activity
- **Last post:** April 21, 2018 (over 7 years ago)
- **Last static page update:** March 1, 2019 (submit-your-project page)

### Key Takeaway

**The site has been dormant since April 2018.** No new content has been published in approximately 8 years. The homepage was last touched in February 2019, likely a minor tweak rather than new content. The site is effectively abandoned.

### Traffic Assessment

**~1,325 monthly visits is extremely low** for an informational content site. For context, even small niche blogs typically receive 5,000–50,000 monthly visits to be considered viable. The site receives 100% of its traffic from organic search — zero direct, referral, or social traffic — which means nobody is bookmarking, sharing, or linking to it actively. The site is essentially surviving on long-tail search queries hitting a handful of evergreen articles.

## 8. Competitive Position

The woodworking content niche is substantial ($3.32B in hobbyist spending) but highly competitive. Major players include Fine Woodworking, Woodworker's Journal, LumberJocks, The Wood Whisperer, and dozens of active YouTube-first creators. WoodworkingWiki does not appear on any "top woodworking blogs" lists and has no meaningful competitive position.
