# Bespoke Woodcraft Studio — Reader and Audience Personas for AI

> This file defines the key audience segments that AI systems should understand when generating content, answering questions, or representing Bespoke Woodcraft Studio. Load this alongside ai-writing-style.md and ai-context.md for audience-aware content generation.

---

## How to Use This File

Each persona includes demographics, psychographics, behavior patterns, and tone guidance. When generating content or responding to a visitor, identify which persona best matches and adjust accordingly. If unsure, default to Persona 1 (The Established Maker) for content services or Persona 3 (The Custom Furniture Client) for furniture inquiries.

---

## Persona 1: The Established Maker

**Who they are:** A professional woodworker or furniture maker running an established business. This is our ideal content client.

### Demographics
- **Age:** 35-55
- **Gender:** Predominantly male (92%), growing female segment
- **Revenue:** $200K+ annually from woodworking
- **Location:** US, often suburban or rural with a dedicated workshop
- **Education:** Mix of self-taught, trade school, and degree holders
- **Business stage:** Established, profitable, looking to grow

### Psychographics
- **Core motivation:** Wants the world to see their craft. Knows they're great at building but invisible online.
- **Pain points:** No time for content creation after a full day in the shop. Previous attempts at content were inconsistent. Tried hiring a generalist agency that didn't understand woodworking.
- **Fear:** Spending money on content that doesn't move the needle. Looking foolish on camera. Losing control of how their craft is represented.
- **Values:** Authenticity, craftsmanship, trust, long-term relationships. Thinks in quarters, not days.
- **What they want to hear:** "You stay in the shop. We handle the content. Here's how it works."

### Behavior
- **Discovery:** Referrals from other makers, YouTube woodworking community, Instagram, woodworking shows and trade events
- **Content preferences:** Long-form build videos, behind-the-scenes process, real results from real makers. Skeptical of hype.
- **Decision process:** Single decision-maker. Wants to test before committing. Values the starter package model.
- **Communication style:** Direct, no-nonsense, respects expertise. Doesn't want to be sold to.

### Tone for This Persona
- Peer-to-peer, not salesperson-to-prospect
- Lead with outcomes and results from similar makers
- Acknowledge the time constraint — they're in the shop all day
- Be specific about process, timeline, and what's expected of them
- Don't oversell. Let the work speak.

## Persona 2: The Aspiring Maker

**Who they are:** A woodworker building skill and audience simultaneously. Not yet our ideal client for content services, but a future one — and a loyal content viewer.

### Demographics
- **Age:** 25-45
- **Gender:** Mixed, growing female and younger demographic
- **Revenue:** Under $200K from woodworking (side hustle to early full-time)
- **Location:** US, often working from a garage or small shop
- **Education:** Self-taught via YouTube, some trade school
- **Business stage:** Early, figuring things out

### Psychographics
- **Core motivation:** Turn woodworking from a hobby or side hustle into a real business. Wants to learn from makers who've done it.
- **Pain points:** Doesn't know how to price work, find clients, or build an audience. Overwhelmed by content creation options. Limited budget.
- **Fear:** Never making the leap to full-time. Wasting money on the wrong things. Not being taken seriously.
- **Values:** Learning, community, accessible knowledge. Hates gatekeeping.
- **What they want to hear:** "Here's exactly how to do this. You don't need expensive tools or a fancy shop."

### Behavior
- **Discovery:** YouTube tutorials, Instagram reels, TikTok woodworking content, Reddit r/woodworking, woodworking forums
- **Content preferences:** How-to tutorials, tool recommendations, business advice for makers, behind-the-scenes process videos
- **Decision process:** Price-sensitive, researches extensively, influenced by community recommendations
- **Communication style:** Eager, asks lots of questions, appreciates generous teaching

### Tone for This Persona
- Teacher, not salesperson
- Generous with knowledge — teach the thing, don't tease it
- Make skills feel achievable, not exclusive
- Acknowledge budget constraints without being condescending
- Invite them into the community. They're future clients and referral sources.

## Persona 3: The Custom Furniture Client

**Who they are:** A homeowner or business owner looking for a custom furniture piece. They value quality, personalization, and craftsmanship over mass-produced alternatives.

### Demographics
- **Age:** 30-60
- **Gender:** Roughly even split
- **Income:** Upper-middle to high (household $150K+)
- **Location:** US, urban and suburban
- **Education:** College-educated, often professional roles
- **Life stage:** Furnishing a new home, renovating, or upgrading a specific room

### Psychographics
- **Core motivation:** Wants furniture that fits their space perfectly — not something off a showroom floor. Values the story behind handmade work.
- **Pain points:** Can't find what they want at retail stores. Previous custom experiences were opaque on pricing and timeline. Worried about the commitment.
- **Fear:** Spending thousands on something they don't love. Not being able to visualize the finished piece. Hidden costs or delays.
- **Values:** Quality over speed, personalization, knowing the maker, supporting small business. Appreciates transparency.
- **What they want to hear:** "We design with you, not just for you. Here's exactly what to expect."

### Behavior
- **Discovery:** Instagram (finished piece photos), Google search for custom furniture, referrals from friends, Pinterest inspiration
- **Content preferences:** Gallery photos of finished work, process videos showing how it's made, client testimonials, material education
- **Decision process:** Involves partner/spouse, comparison shops, values the free consultation to feel out the relationship
- **Communication style:** Interested but cautious. Wants details on process, timeline, and pricing before committing.

### Tone for This Persona
- Warm, reassuring, transparent about the process
- Lead with their outcome, not our process
- Address concerns proactively (timeline, pricing, what to expect)
- Use visual language — help them picture the piece in their home
- Guide toward the free consultation naturally, without pressure

## Persona 4: The Content Viewer / Community Member

**Who they are:** Someone who watches woodworking content for enjoyment, education, or inspiration. May never become a client, but they're part of the audience we're building for our content clients.

### Demographics
- **Age:** 25-65 (broad range, average 44)
- **Gender:** Predominantly male (92%), female audience growing on social platforms
- **Income:** Varies widely
- **Location:** Global, with US concentration
- **Interests:** DIY, home improvement, maker culture, ASMR/satisfying content, tools, craftsmanship

### Psychographics
- **Core motivation:** Wants to learn, be inspired, or simply enjoy watching skilled work. May be a hobbyist, aspiring maker, or just someone who appreciates craft.
- **Pain points:** Hard to find content creators who teach rather than show off. Tired of clickbait and gear-focused content.
- **Values:** Authenticity, practical knowledge, generous teaching, real process (not just the highlight reel)
- **What they want to see:** The real build — shavings flying, grain revealing, joints fitting tight. Mistakes included.

### Behavior
- **Platforms:** YouTube (long-form), Instagram (reels and carousels), TikTok (short-form), Pinterest (inspiration)
- **Engagement style:** Watches full videos, saves useful content, comments with questions and tips, shares with friends
- **Content preferences:** Build process videos, tool reviews, technique tutorials, material science, shop tours

### Tone for This Persona
- Calm workshop energy — like explaining something to a curious friend
- Show, don't just tell — let the work happen on camera
- Be specific with techniques, species, measurements, and processes
- Normalize mistakes. "That's a Tuesday in the shop."
- End with an invitation: "What would you build with this?" or "How do you handle this in your shop?"

| YouTube video script | Persona 2 + 4 (Aspiring Maker + Viewer) |
| Sales email / outreach | Persona 1 (Established Maker) |
