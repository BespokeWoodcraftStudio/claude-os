## What This Is

This folder contains curated indexes of sources you trust and people you respect. These are the foundations for how you think about building companies, leading teams, writing, and staying current.

**Why this matters:**
- Knowing your sources forces clarity about your mental models
- Curated sources accelerate learning (avoid noise)
- Shared with the team, this creates alignment on "what good looks like"
- When evaluating new information, check it against trusted sources

---

## How This Is Organized

### [people-index.md](./people-index.md)
People whose thinking you trust and follow. Organized by domain:
- **Operators & CEOs** -- People who've built and scaled companies
- **Writers & Communicators** -- Craft of clear thinking and writing
- **Investors & Advisors** -- Pattern recognition across companies
- **AI & Technology** -- Technical leaders shaping the field
- **Growth & Marketing** -- Demand generation and go-to-market
- **Strategy & Management** -- Frameworks for organizational effectiveness

### [sources-index.md](./sources-index.md)
Publications, newsletters, podcasts, books, and tools you return to regularly. Organized by type:
- **Newsletters & Blogs** -- Regular reading
- **Podcasts** -- Deep dives and interviews
- **Books** -- Foundational texts
- **Publications & Research** -- Institutional knowledge
- **Tools & Platforms** -- Reference data and benchmarks
- **Communities & Forums** -- Peer learning

---

## How to Use This

**For decision-making:** When facing a new challenge, check if anyone on the people list has written/spoken about it.

**For learning:** Pick 5-10 newsletters from the sources list for your regular reading rotation.

**For hiring:** Share relevant sections with candidates to signal what thinking you value.

**For team development:** Point team members to specific sources for their growth areas.

---

## Maintenance

Review quarterly:
- Add new sources that have proven valuable
- Remove sources that have declined in quality
- Promote people who've become more relevant
- Note which sources are most frequently referenced

---

## Selection Criteria

**For people:**
- Track record of building or advising at scale
- Original thinking (not just summarizing others)
- Willingness to share specifics, not just platitudes
- Consistency over time (not one-hit wonders)

**For sources:**
- Signal-to-noise ratio (high value per piece)
- Specificity and actionability
- Relevant to your stage and challenges
- Updated regularly (for time-sensitive topics)
