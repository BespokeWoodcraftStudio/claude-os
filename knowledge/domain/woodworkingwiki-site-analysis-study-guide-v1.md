## Executive Summary

WoodworkingWiki.com is a small, abandoned WordPress content site in the woodworking niche. Launched in February 2015 by a solo creator named Steve Squires, the site published just 22 posts before going dormant in April 2018. It currently receives approximately 1,325 monthly page visits — almost entirely from long-tail organic search — and ranks 12,032,730 globally on SimilarWeb. Despite claiming 122,000+ Instagram followers, the site generates zero social traffic. It has no competitive relevance in the woodworking content landscape and shows no signs of revival.

---

## 1. Site Profile

WoodworkingWiki.com describes itself as "your central hub for everything related to woodworking," offering how-to articles, free plans, video tutorials, tool reviews, and community content. In practice, it's a modest WordPress blog with 22 posts and 9 static pages — far from the comprehensive resource its "wiki" branding implies.

The site runs on WordPress with the Newspaper theme by tagDiv, uses Yoast SEO for search optimization, and monetizes through Google AdSense and affiliate marketing. The contact email is admin@woodworkingwiki.com. The About page conspicuously avoids naming anyone behind the project.

The primary (and likely sole) author is Steve Squires, who has no significant web presence beyond this site and a dormant Medium profile with one post from 2017. No team, contributors, or editorial structure is evident.

---

## 2. Monthly Traffic

Based on SimilarWeb data as of February 2026:

**The site receives approximately 1,325 monthly visits.** This is extremely low for a content site — most viable niche blogs generate between 10,000 and 100,000 monthly visits to sustain even modest ad revenue. For additional context, the site's global rank is 12,032,730 and its US rank is 3,646,466, placing it in the deep tail of the internet.

Engagement metrics tell a similar story. The bounce rate sits at roughly 41%, and visitors view about 1.06 pages per session — meaning almost everyone who arrives reads one page and leaves. Average visit duration was too low for SimilarWeb to report.

All traffic comes from organic search. The breakdown is stark: 100% organic, 0% direct, 0% social, 0% referral, 0% paid. Nobody bookmarks the site. Nobody shares its content on social media. Nobody links to it from other sites. It survives purely on Google sending long-tail searchers to a handful of evergreen posts.

The top search queries driving traffic are specific product and technique searches: "dewalt 780," "dovetail definition," "dewalt dws780 height from bottom to deck," and "best way to apply non-hardening wood putty." These are informational queries where Google surfaces old but still-relevant content.

## 3. Content Inventory & Update Frequency

### Total Content

The site contains 22 posts and 9 static pages (31 URLs total), confirmed via its XML sitemap. Content categories include articles (general woodworking topics), reviews (tool evaluations), tips and tricks, and one glossary entry.

### Publishing Timeline

The site's publishing history reveals an erratic, ultimately abandoned effort:

**Phase 1 — Launch burst (February–August 2015):** Nine posts published in the first six months. Topics ranged from flooring reviews to DIY projects. This was the site's most productive period.

**The long gap (August 2015–June 2017):** Approximately 22 months of complete silence. No new posts, no updates.

**Phase 2 — Bulk update (June 18, 2017):** Nine posts were modified on a single day. This was likely a bulk republish or SEO refresh rather than new content creation — a common tactic for trying to revive stale content.

**Phase 3 — Final push (February–April 2018):** Four new posts published over three months, focused on tool reviews (DeWalt miter saw, best miter saws) and practical articles (wood putty vs. filler, reconditioning batteries).

**Last post:** April 21, 2018 — "Wood Putty vs Wood Filler"

**Last site activity:** March 1, 2019 — a minor update to the "Submit Your Project" page

**The site has not been updated in over 7 years.** It is effectively abandoned. No new content, no maintenance, no community engagement.

## 4. Social Media Presence

The site footer claims the following social media presence:

- Instagram (@woodworkingwiki): 122,309 followers
- Twitter: 4,292 followers
- Facebook: 350 likes
- Pinterest: present (no count available)
- Google+: listed (service shut down April 2019)

The Instagram follower count of 122,000+ is the standout anomaly. SimilarWeb shows precisely 0% of site traffic coming from social media, which means those 122K followers are either not clicking through to the website, the account has been repurposed for different content, or the followers are not genuine. For a site with 1,325 monthly visits and 22 total posts, a six-figure Instagram following defies explanation without one of those factors. The presence of Google+ in the footer confirms the site hasn't been meaningfully updated since at least early 2019.

## 5. SEO & Technical Profile

The site uses standard WordPress SEO infrastructure: Yoast SEO plugin, XML sitemaps, and Google Analytics. It runs Google AdSense (still serving ads on dormant content) and employs 20 technologies across 9 categories per SimilarWeb's technology analysis.

WHOIS data is privacy-protected or unavailable — a lookup on who.is returned no results.

The site's organic search performance is minimal. It ranks for a handful of low-competition, long-tail keywords related to specific tools (DeWalt DWS780) and techniques (dovetail joints, wood putty). It has no meaningful domain authority in any third-party tracker and does not appear on any "top woodworking blogs" or industry resource lists.

## 6. Competitive Position

WoodworkingWiki.com has no competitive relevance in the woodworking content space. The niche is dominated by established players including Fine Woodworking, Woodworker's Journal, The Wood Whisperer, LumberJocks, and dozens of active YouTube-first creators. The woodworking hobby market represents $3.32 billion in annual spending, but WoodworkingWiki captures none of this opportunity in any meaningful way.

The domain name itself — woodworkingwiki.com — carries inherent keyword value and memorability. However, the site behind it has done nothing to earn authority, build an audience, or create a content moat. With only 22 posts across 3 years of sporadic activity and 7+ years of dormancy, the domain is arguably more valuable than the content on it.

---

## 7. Key Takeaways

**For traffic:** The site gets roughly 1,325 visits per month, all from organic search. This is negligible — equivalent to about 44 visitors per day. It has no social, direct, or referral traffic whatsoever.

**For update frequency:** The site has not published new content since April 2018. It is dormant and shows no signs of revival.

**For content volume:** 22 posts in total. This is far below the volume needed for a "wiki" or even a modest blog to gain traction in search.

**For the operator:** Steve Squires appears to have been a solo operator who attempted a content site, couldn't sustain publishing, and walked away. The site remains online (likely due to hosting being auto-renewed or paid in advance) but receives no active management.

**For Bespoke Woodcraft Studio:** This site represents what happens when a content project launches without sustained commitment. The domain name is strong, the niche is right, but 22 posts over 3 years with no consistency is not enough to build anything. The 122K Instagram followers juxtaposed against 1,325 monthly site visits is a useful case study in the gap between vanity metrics and actual audience engagement.
