## Part 5: YouTube Video Strategy — The Playbook

This section is the core strategic guide for building a YouTube channel around woodworking content.

### Channel Architecture

**Primary content pillars (pick 2-3 to start):**
1. Project builds (your signature builds)
2. Educational tutorials (techniques, tools, finishing)
3. Behind-the-scenes / business of woodworking

**Content calendar template:**
- Monday: Post weekly long-form video (12-20 min)
- Tuesday-Thursday: Post 1-2 Shorts from Monday's build footage
- Friday: Post a Short (standalone tip or tool content)
- Daily: Engage with comments for 15-20 minutes

### Video Production Framework

**Pre-production:**
- Choose topic from ranked list above (prioritize top 10 for first 6 months)
- Write a 1-sentence hook for the first 30 seconds
- Plan 3-5 "clip moments" during the build that can become Shorts, TikToks, and Reels
- Prepare thumbnail concept before filming (shoot thumbnail-specific angles during build)

**Filming:**
- A-roll: Main build footage (tripod, good lighting, overhead shots)
- B-roll: Close-ups of cuts, joints, finishing (handheld or macro lens)
- ASMR shots: Raw audio of tools on wood (no music overlay)
- Reaction moments: Film your genuine reaction to grain reveals, first coat, final assembly
- Vertical clips: Flip phone vertical for 3-5 key moments (for Shorts/TikTok/Reels)

**Post-production:**
- Edit long-form first (12-20 minutes)
- Pull 5-8 short clips (30-60 seconds each) for repurposing
- Add text overlays to shorts (many viewers watch muted)
- Create thumbnail: close-up of finished piece, bright/warm lighting, 3-4 words max

### Title & Thumbnail Formulas

**Titles that work:**
- "[Project] — [Emotional Hook]" → "Building a River Table — I Can't Believe How This Turned Out"
- "How to [Technique] — [Benefit]" → "How to Cut Perfect Dovetails — The Method Nobody Teaches"
- "[Challenge] [Project]" → "$100 Workshop Challenge — Full Furniture Build"
- "I Built a [Project] and [Unexpected Result]" → "I Built a Dining Table and My Wife Cried"

**Thumbnail principles:**
- Show the finished piece, not the process
- Bright, warm lighting (workshop aesthetic)
- Your face showing genuine emotion (amazement, pride) increases CTR by 30%+
- 3-4 words max of text — contrast color against background
- Before/after split frame for transformation content

### Growth Strategy

**Phase 1 (Months 1-3): Foundation**
- Post 1 long-form + 3-5 Shorts per week consistently
- Focus on top 5 topics from the ranked list
- Optimize titles and thumbnails based on CTR data
- Build a "gateway video" — one beginner project tutorial designed to be the entry point

**Phase 2 (Months 4-6): Cross-Platform**
- Repurpose Shorts to TikTok and Instagram Reels
- Start building email list from YouTube descriptions
- Collaborate with 1-2 other woodworking creators
- Experiment with trending formats (ASMR, fails, reveals)

**Phase 3 (Months 7-12): Monetization & Authority**
- Apply for YouTube Partner Program (1,000 subs + 4,000 watch hours)
- Add affiliate links for tools featured in videos
- Create a "signature series" (recurring build format that becomes your brand)
- Launch behind-the-scenes content (workshop vlogs, business of woodworking)

### Monetization Paths

| Revenue Stream | When to Start | Expected Range |
|----------------|--------------|----------------|
| YouTube Ad Revenue | 1,000 subs / 4K watch hours | $3-$6 CPM ($3,000-$6,000 per 1M views) |

### Repurposing Workflow

For every long-form YouTube video, extract:

1. **3-5 YouTube Shorts** (30-45 sec each)
   - Before/after transformation
   - Most satisfying moment (cut, pour, reveal)
   - Quick tip or technique highlight
   - Mistake/fix moment
   - Finished piece showcase

2. **3-5 TikToks** (30-60 sec each)
   - Same clips as Shorts, re-edited with trending TikTok audio
   - Add TikTok-native text overlays and captions
   - Adjust hook for TikTok audience (more personality, less polish)

3. **3-5 Instagram Reels** (7-30 sec each)
   - Tighter edits than TikTok — Instagram favors shorter
   - Use trending Instagram audio (different from TikTok trends)
   - Focus on the most visually striking moments

4. **1-2 Instagram Carousels**
   - Process photos: raw material → key build stages → finished piece
   - Tool/technique breakdown with text on each slide
   - Design inspiration: sketches, material choices, final result

5. **Instagram Stories** (daily during build week)
   - Behind-the-scenes footage
   - Polls ("Which finish should I use?")
   - Progress updates
