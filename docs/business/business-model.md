- We sell a starter video package (1 month) that converts to ongoing monthly retainers. Target annual client value: $36k-$60k.
- Five growth levers: more discovery calls, better starter-to-retainer conversion, higher monthly retainer value, lower churn, and expanding services within existing clients.

## Why This Model Exists

Most woodworkers and makers are incredible at their craft but invisible online. They know they need video and social content to grow, but the alternatives are bad: hire a generalist videographer who doesn't understand the trade, try to DIY content after a full day in the shop, or post inconsistently and wonder why nothing grows.

**Our model combines:**

- Deep understanding of the woodworking and maker space — we speak the language
- Done-for-you video production and content creation, so makers stay in the shop
- Monthly retainers that build momentum instead of one-off projects that fizzle
- A content system that compounds — each month's work builds on the last

---

## The Business Model

A new client typically finds us through social media, referrals from other woodworkers, or our own content. We start with a discovery call to understand their business and goals. If there's a fit, we offer a **Starter Package** — a single-month engagement (usually 1-2 shoot days plus editing and distribution) priced at $2,500-$4,000. This lets both sides test the working relationship with real deliverables.

When the starter goes well — and it usually does — we transition the client to a **Monthly Retainer** at $3,000-$5,000/month. Retainers include a set number of shoot days, edited videos, short-form cuts for social, and content strategy. The retainer model means we're not constantly re-selling; we're building a library of content that grows the client's audience month over month.

## The Revenue Math

Revenue growth is a function of how many retainer clients we carry and how long they stay.

**Inputs:**
- Number of discovery calls per month
- Conversion rate from discovery call to starter package
- Conversion rate from starter to monthly retainer
- Average retainer duration (months)

**The formula:**

```
Monthly Revenue = (Active Retainer Clients x Avg Monthly Retainer) + (New Starter Packages x Avg Starter Price)

Net Client Growth = New Retainer Clients Added - Clients Churned
```

### Growth Levers

There are only a few ways to grow faster:

1. **More discovery calls** — Increase inbound through our own content, woodworking community presence, and referrals
2. **Better starter conversion** — Make the starter package so good that saying no to a retainer feels like leaving money on the table
3. **Higher retainer value** — Add services (photography, email newsletters, website content) that increase monthly spend per client
4. **Lower churn** — Keep clients longer by showing measurable audience and business growth each month
5. **Expand within clients** — Offer additional brands, product launches, or seasonal campaigns to existing retainer clients

---

## Why Churn Matters

Content compounds. A client who stays 12 months has a library of videos, a growing audience, and real momentum. A client who leaves after 3 months got a handful of nice videos but never hit the flywheel.

The math is unforgiving: if we lose even 10% of retainer clients per month, we lose 72% of our base annually. At 5% monthly churn, we lose 46% annually. Our target is under 5% monthly churn — meaning most clients stay at least 18-24 months.

Every churned client also costs us the referral they would have sent. Woodworking is a tight community. One happy maker telling three friends is our best growth channel.

## Gross Margins

Target gross margin is 60%+. COGS includes videographer and editor time (contract or staff), travel to client shops, gear maintenance, and software (editing suites, hosting, scheduling tools).

**What improves margins:**
- Reusable templates and editing workflows that speed up post-production
- Batching shoot days (filming 2-3 videos in a single visit)
- Clients in geographic clusters that reduce travel time and cost

**What hurts margins:**
- Clients in remote locations requiring significant travel
- High revision cycles from clients who struggle to articulate what they want
- Scope creep — doing extra shoots or edits outside the retainer without adjusting pricing

---

## Why the Model Scales

Three structural advantages compound over time:

1. **Content library effect.** Every month of work adds to a client's content library. The more we've shot, the easier it is to repurpose, remix, and build on existing footage. Month 12 is more efficient than month 1.

2. **Community-driven referrals.** Woodworkers know other woodworkers. A strong client portfolio in the maker space generates warm introductions that cost us nothing to acquire.

3. **Systematized production.** As we work with more woodworkers, we build repeatable shot lists, editing templates, and content calendars specific to the trade. This institutional knowledge makes every new client faster to onboard and serve.
