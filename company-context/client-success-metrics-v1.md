## TL;DR

- Five dimensions: Output, Quality, Performance, Relationship, Strategy. All must be strong.
- Leading signals (fixable early): declining communication, revision requests up, questions about cost.
- Lagging signals (often too late): shopping alternatives, formal complaints, champion leaves.
- Best accounts: they want to hire our people. Worst accounts: conversations about article counts.

## Overview

Client health comes down to five things. If all five are strong, renewals are inevitable. If any are weak, we're at risk.

Track these internally. Ask directly at least quarterly.

---

## The Five Dimensions

### 1. Output

**Question:** Are we doing the work?

- Are we publishing content?
- Creating new pages?
- Refreshing existing pages?
- Are we doing the activities required to drive results?

**This is table stakes.** If we're not producing, nothing else matters.

**Warning signs:**
- Missed deadlines
- Falling behind on content calendar
- Backlog growing instead of shrinking

---

### 2. Quality

**Question:** Is the work good?

- Is the quality acceptable?
- Is it actually good?
- Would this content ruin their reputation or build it?
- Are we seeing engagement?

**Quality has layers:**
- Accuracy (facts correct, no errors)
- Voice (sounds like them, not generic)
- Depth (substantive, not thin)
- Strategy (targeting the right topics)
- Polish (no typos, well-formatted)

**Warning signs:**
- Frequent revision requests
- Customer editing our work heavily
- Complaints about quality
- Content not performing

---

### 3. Performance

**Question:** Is it working?

- Is the content driving organic growth?
- Is AI visibility improving?
- Are we achieving the goals we set?
- Are we improving or decaying?
- Do they understand the results we're driving?

**The outcome chain:**
1. **Published** → Did it go live?
2. **Indexed** → Did Google/LLMs see it?
3. **Impressions** → Is it showing up in results?
4. **Clicks/Traffic** → Are people visiting?
5. **Conversions** → Are they taking action?

**Warning signs:**
- Flat or declining traffic
- No indexation
- No visibility improvement
- Customer doesn't know what's working

---

### 4. Relationship

**Question:** Do they love working with us?

- Would they sign up again if they had to choose today?
- Are they referring other people?
- Are they planning to renew?
- Are they asking us to do more?

**Strong relationship signals:**
- They ask to expand scope
- They refer other companies
- They defend us internally
- They're responsive and engaged
- They treat us like partners, not vendors

**Weak relationship signals:**
- Hard to get on calls
- Short, transactional communication
- Questioning costs and output counts
- Comparing us to alternatives
- Executive sponsor disengaged

---

### 5. Strategy

**Question:** Do they know where we're heading?

- Do they understand the strategy?
- Is the direction clear and communicated?
- Are they excited and bought in?
- Does the executive stakeholder (the one who signs the contract) understand the ROI?

**Strategy alignment means:**
- Main point of contact is bought in
- Executive sponsor is bought in
- They see the big picture, not just weekly deliverables
- They understand why we're prioritizing what we're prioritizing

**Warning signs:**
- Confusion about priorities
- Frequent direction changes
- "What's the plan?" questions
- Disconnect between POC and executive

## Leading vs Lagging Signals

Not all signals are equal. Some tell you early. Some tell you too late.

### Leading Signals (Early Warning)

- Communication quality declining
- Responsiveness dropping
- Revision requests increasing
- Questions about cost or output
- New stakeholders not engaged
- Missing calls or reschedules

**These are fixable if you catch them early.**

### Lagging Signals (Often Too Late)

- Renewal conversation goes poorly
- They're shopping alternatives
- Budget cuts announced
- Champion leaves the company
- Formal complaints

**By the time you see these, you're in recovery mode.**

## What to Ask and When

### Weekly (Informal)

- How are things going?
- Anything we should know about?
- What's top of mind for you this week?

### Monthly (Check-in)

- Are you happy with the quality?
- Is the work tracking toward your goals?
- Any feedback on how we're working together?

### Quarterly (Formal Review)

- Would you become a customer again if you had a choice today?
- Are you planning to renew?
- What would make this engagement even more valuable?
- How are we tracking against the goals we set?
- Is there anything we should be doing differently?

## Account Management

For every account in Growth Execution, you should be doing these things:

### Map the Organization

- Who are all the stakeholders?
- What are the dynamics between them?
- Who's the champion? Who's the skeptic?
- Who signs the contract?
- Who influences the decision?

### Keep Tabs on Competitors

- What are their competitors doing?
- Are competitors working with agencies like us?
- What's the competitive pressure they're facing?

### Create FOMO

- Share what other customers are achieving
- Show industry benchmarks
- Highlight what they could be doing but aren't

### Run QBRs

- Quarterly business reviews are mandatory
- Review performance against goals
- Present the roadmap for next quarter
- Get explicit buy-in on direction

### Use Every Touchpoint

Every interaction is an opportunity to:
- Build trust
- Sell the value we're delivering
- Surface concerns before they become problems
- Deepen the relationship

## Signs of a Healthy Account

Ask yourself these questions:

- [ ] Is this a customer we brag about publicly?
- [ ] Can we show growth charts?
- [ ] Are they referring business to us?
- [ ] Are they asking us to increase scope?
- [ ] Do you look forward to the weekly call?
- [ ] Do you feel like friends with their team?
- [ ] Do they wish they could poach our people?

**If most of these are yes, you have a healthy account.**

---

## Signs of an Unhealthy Account

- They're asking about cost and output counts
- You dread the calls
- They're flagging issues before we do
- The conversation is transactional, not strategic
- Communication has dropped off
- They're not responsive
- New stakeholders are skeptical

**If you see these, you're at risk. Escalate and build a recovery plan.**

## How We Help Clients Prove ROI

Ultimately, they need to show their leadership that working with us drives business value.

### The Chain We Help Them Build

1. **Content → Visibility**
   We create content that gets indexed and ranks

2. **Visibility → Traffic**
   Rankings drive clicks and visitors

3. **Traffic → Conversions**
   Visitors take action (signup, demo, purchase)

4. **Conversions → Revenue**
   Actions turn into pipeline and closed deals

### Our Responsibility

We own steps 1-2 completely. We influence step 3. We advise on step 4.

We build the engine that drives high-quality traffic. We help them think about conversion. We don't do conversion rate optimization.

### What They Need to Have

For ROI to be provable, they need:
- Conversion tracking set up
- At least one compelling offer that works
- Clear paths from content to conversion
- Attribution model (even simple)

**If they don't have this, help them fix it.** Otherwise they can't prove the value of any initiative, ours or anyone else's.
