## Creator Profiles (Ranked by Influence × Storytelling Quality)

### 1. Ishitani Furniture (Natsuki Ishitani)

- **Platform:** YouTube
- **Subscribers:** ~1M+
- **Style:** Japanese master furniture maker. Videos are silent construction documentaries — no narration, just the sound of tools and wood. Filmed by his wife Chie. Mixes traditional Japanese techniques with modern Festool equipment.
- **Representative quote/approach:** *Silence is the voice.* His work speaks entirely through craft — the rhythm of a hand plane, the precision of a mortise. No words needed.
- **Archetype:** **The Silent Master**
- **Why he matters for how-to voice:** Proves that authority comes from demonstrated mastery, not explanation. The anti-voice that reminds us: sometimes the craft itself is the story.
- **Source:** machineatlas.com, YouTube

---

### 2. Paul Sellers

- **Platform:** YouTube, Blog (paulsellers.com)
- **Subscribers:** ~689K
- **Style:** The global authority on hand tool woodworking. Pioneered the resurgence of hand tools. Content is methodical, patient, deeply educational. Focuses on beginners. British warmth with decades of workshop wisdom.
- **Representative quote:** *"I'm not trying to make woodworking look easy. I'm trying to make it look possible."* (paraphrased from interviews)
- **Archetype:** **The Patient Master**
- **Why he matters for how-to voice:** The gold standard for written how-to tone — patient, precise, never condescending. His voice says: "I've done this ten thousand times, and I'm going to walk you through it like it's the first."
- **Source:** Wikipedia, Woodworking Masterclasses

---

### 3. Blacktail Studio (Cam Anderson)

- **Platform:** YouTube, Instagram (@blacktailstudio)
- **Subscribers:** ~3.4M
- **Style:** High-production content focused on live-edge and epoxy river tables. Former emergency transport pilot. Selects retail projects based on storytelling and video potential, not just profit. Films on iPhone, edits with $9 app.
- **Representative quote:** *"I don't choose projects based on what will sell. I choose them based on what will make a good story."* (paraphrased from Tubefilter interview)
- **Archetype:** **The Narrative Craftsman**
- **Why he matters for how-to voice:** Shows that every project has a story arc. His content teaches that the "why" behind a build is as compelling as the "how."
- **Source:** Tubefilter, Wagner Meters

---

### 4. Steve Ramsey (Woodworking for Mere Mortals)

- **Platform:** YouTube
- **Subscribers:** ~1.89M
- **Style:** Simplifies woodworking for complete beginners. Charismatic, warm, self-deprecating humor. Focuses on projects people can actually build without big shops or expensive tools.
- **Representative quote:** *"You don't need a $3,000 table saw. You need a $40 circular saw and the confidence to use it."* (characteristic of his approach)
- **Archetype:** **The Friendly Democratizer**
- **Why he matters for how-to voice:** The master of "you can do this" energy. His voice removes intimidation without removing substance. Perfect model for beginner-facing how-to articles.
- **Source:** Social Blade, Starter Story

---

### 5. Essential Craftsman (Scott Wadsworth)

- **Platform:** YouTube
- **Subscribers:** ~1.25M
- **Style:** Philosophy-driven content about blacksmithing, carpentry, and masonry. 40+ years of trade experience (since 1974). Content transcends instruction — it's about the meaning and dignity of manual work.
- **Representative quote:** *"Everything civilization required emerged between the face of an anvil and the face of a hammer."*
- **Archetype:** **The Philosophical Tradesman**
- **Why he matters for how-to voice:** Brings moral weight to craft instruction. His voice says: "This matters. What we build with our hands matters." Elevates how-to from tutorial to philosophy.
- **Source:** essentialcraftsman.com, Engineer Fix

---

### 6. Bourbon Moth Woodworking (Jason Hibbs)

- **Platform:** YouTube, Instagram (@bourbonmoth)
- **Subscribers:** ~1.24M
- **Style:** Started in an Oregon garage. Humor-driven, self-deprecating, authentic. 90% of builds come from audience suggestions. Explicitly rejects algorithmic pressure — films on iPhone, edits cheaply. "Aesthetic sincerity" over production polish.
- **Representative quote:** *"I'm not a master woodworker. I'm a guy who makes stuff and sometimes it works out."* (characteristic of his voice)
- **Archetype:** **The Honest Maker**
- **Why he matters for how-to voice:** Proves that vulnerability and humor build trust faster than expertise alone. His voice gives permission to fail — essential for how-to content.
- **Source:** NetInfluencer, bourbonmoth.com

### 7. Pask Makes (Neil Paskin)

- **Platform:** YouTube, Instagram (@PaskMakes)
- **Subscribers:** ~1.2M
- **Style:** Australian maker who uses stop-motion animation to narrate builds. Range includes Kumiko, wooden fans, kayaks. Each video is a "film" not a tutorial. Poetic, minimalist narration. Committed to giving "his all" to each project.
- **Representative quote:** *"I don't claim to be an expert. I'm learning through doing, and I want to take you along."* (paraphrased from interviews)
- **Archetype:** **The Poetic Explorer**
- **Why he matters for how-to voice:** Models the "learning in public" voice. His content says: "We're figuring this out together." Powerful for how-to articles that acknowledge uncertainty.
- **Source:** paskmakes.com, Passionpedia

### 8. Foureyes Furniture (Chris Salomone)

- **Platform:** YouTube, Instagram (@foureyesfurniture)
- **Subscribers:** ~1.4M
- **Style:** Furniture designer-engineer making sleek, modern, architecturally precise pieces. Pioneered "narrative filmmaking" approach to woodworking YouTube — macro shots, environmental wides, improvised guitar soundtracks. Broke the mold of talking-head tutorials.
- **Representative quote:** *"I wanted the videos to feel like something, not just teach something."* (paraphrased from Cal State Fullerton profile)
- **Archetype:** **The Design Storyteller**
- **Why he matters for how-to voice:** Proves that design thinking and emotional resonance belong in instructional content. His voice treats the reader/viewer as aesthetically intelligent.
- **Source:** Cal State Fullerton, NoxInfluencer

### 9. Samurai Carpenter (Jesse de Geest)

- **Platform:** YouTube, Instagram (@samuraicarpenter)

- **Representative quote:** *"The old ways aren't old because they stopped working. They're old because they've been working for centuries."* (characteristic of his philosophy)
- **Archetype:** **The Bridge Builder**

### 10. Marc Spagnuolo (The Wood Whisperer)

- **Platform:** YouTube, Instagram (@woodwhisperer)
- **Subscribers:** ~850K (YouTube), ~300K (Instagram)
- **Style:** First online video series about woodworking (started 2006). Fine furniture making with emphasis on clear instruction at all skill levels. Professional, accessible, with dry humor. Built sustainable education business from content.
- **Representative quote:** *"Good woodworking isn't about perfection. It's about understanding what the wood wants to do and working with it."* (characteristic of his teaching style)
- **Archetype:** **The Professional Mentor**
- **Why he matters for how-to voice:** The benchmark for instructional clarity paired with professional warmth. His voice is what a great woodworking teacher sounds like in writing: knowledgeable, patient, never showing off.
- **Source:** thewoodwhisperer.com, Klear

## Voice Archetype Framework (from research)

### The 10 Woodworker Voice Archetypes

Based on research across all 20 creators, their voices cluster into 10 distinct archetypes:

| # | Archetype | Core Voice Quality | Best For (How-To) | Example Creators |
|---|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| 1 | The Silent Master | Demonstrated mastery, minimal words | Visual-heavy tutorials, photo essays | Ishitani |
| 2 | The Patient Master | Deep knowledge shared with infinite patience | Beginner fundamentals, hand tool technique | Paul Sellers, Wood Whisperer |
| 3 | The Narrative Craftsman | Every project is a story | Feature articles, project deep-dives | Blacktail Studio, Foureyes |
| 4 | The Friendly Democratizer | "You can do this" energy | Beginner how-tos, budget builds | Steve Ramsey, Rex Krueger |
| 5 | The Philosophical Tradesman | Craft as meaning-making | Opinion pieces, craft philosophy | Essential Craftsman, Offerman |

### Voice Archetype Framework (Jung-Based)

Research into brand archetype theory reveals the most effective woodworking how-to voice blends:

- **Primary: The Sage** — Deep knowledge, credibility, precision, research backing
- **Secondary: The Caregiver** — Warmth, accessibility, patience, reader focus
- **Tertiary: The Creator** — Celebrates making, experimentation, personal expression

This blend produces: **"The expert who has your back"** — knowledgeable but never intimidating, precise but never cold.

### Publication Voice Comparison

| Publication | Archetype Blend | Tone | Audience |
|-------------|----------------|------|----------|
| Fine Woodworking | Sage + Hero | Technical, scholarly, advanced | Serious hobbyists, pros |
| Popular Woodworking | Everyman + Caregiver | Conversational, confidence-building | Intermediate, aspiring |
| Wood Magazine | Creator + Everyman | Project-focused, celebratory | Weekend builders |
