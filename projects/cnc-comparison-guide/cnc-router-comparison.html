Our Recommendations

🏆 Best Overall Value

Sienci AltMill MK2 4×4

The AltMill MK2 delivers industrial-grade rigidity with its all-steel frame, closed-loop NEMA 23 motors, and ball screw drives — all starting at $2,950. With tiling capability for 4×8 sheets and optional high-torque motors and spindle kits, it's the most feature-rich machine in its class. The dust shoe keeps your shop clean, and Sienci's active community and Canadian support are top-notch.

🎯 Best Plug & Play

Onefinity Elite Foreman Gen 2

If you want a full 4×8 bed and minimal assembly, the Elite Foreman Gen 2 is a strong contender — but at $3,837+ (Ultimate package), it's a bigger investment. The Masso G3 controller, ball screws, and included surfacing bit make it genuinely ready to cut out of the box. Just note the bed isn't full 49×49 — it's 48×33 unless you go Foreman size.

💎 Dream Machine (Over Budget)

Sienci AltMill MK2 4×8

Starting at $7,500, the full 4×8 AltMill eliminates tiling entirely with its massive work area. Same industrial-quality construction as the 4×4 with beefier linear rails. If budget allows in the future, this is the endgame for serious woodworkers processing full plywood sheets regularly.

💡 Best Budget Entry

Sienci LongMill MK2.5 48×30

At $2,200, the LongMill gets you cutting quickly with a proven design, great community support, and enough bed size for most projects. It uses lead screws instead of ball screws and open-loop NEMA 23 motors, but for a hobbyist or small shop getting started, it's hard to beat the value.
