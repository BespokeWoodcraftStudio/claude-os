# Bespoke Woodcraft Studio — AI Content Generation Guidelines

> This file defines the rules, guardrails, and best practices for AI-generated content on behalf of Bespoke Woodcraft Studio. Load this alongside ai-writing-style.md, ai-reader-personas.md, and ai-context.md before generating any content.

---

## Purpose

This file ensures that AI-generated content is accurate, on-brand, and safe to publish. It covers what to generate, what to avoid, how to handle edge cases, and how to maintain quality across all content types.

---

## Content Types and Specifications

### Blog Posts

**Length:** 800-1,500 words
**Structure:** Lead with the point, decompose from first principles, connect the pieces, summarize.
**Subheadings:** Every 200-300 words for scannability.
**SEO:** Include primary keyword in title, first paragraph, and one subheading. Write naturally — don't stuff keywords.
**Images:** Reference where images should go (e.g., "[Image: close-up of dovetail joint in white oak]") but do not generate or describe fake images.
**CTA:** One per post, placed naturally at the end. Usually: book a consultation, subscribe to newsletter, or watch a related video.
**Content pillars:** The Craft, The Business of Making, Content for Makers, Lessons from the Shop.

### Social Media Posts

**LinkedIn:**
- Hook must fit within 210-220 characters (before "see more" truncation)
- One sentence per line, generous white space
- 3-4 posts per week
- End with a genuine question (not "Thoughts?")
- No external links in post body (put in comments)
- Best times: Tuesday-Thursday, 9 AM-2 PM EST

**Instagram:**
- Reels: 30-90 seconds, text overlay, process-focused
- Carousels: Step-by-step, before/after, wood selection guides
- Captions: Shorter than LinkedIn, more visual language
- Stories: Behind-the-scenes, polls, design choices

**TikTok:**
- 30-60 seconds
- Process clips, tips, tool demos
- Hook in first 3 seconds
- Show the finished result or surprising moment first, then rewind

**YouTube:**
- Long-form: 10-20 minutes, full build process
- Shorts: 30-60 seconds, tips and process clips
- Hook: First 30 seconds — open with the finished piece or surprising moment, then rewind
- Talk naturally, like explaining to a friend in the shop

**X / Twitter:**
- Short, punchy observations
- Threads for longer ideas
- Quote tweets for industry commentary

### Email Newsletters

**Length:** 300-600 words
**Tone:** Casual, personal — like a note from the shop
**Structure:** One main idea, one CTA
**Subject lines:** Direct, specific, no clickbait. "What I learned from ruining a $400 walnut slab" > "You won't believe what happened!"
**Frequency:** Weekly or bi-weekly

### Client Communications

**Tone:** Professional but warm. Peer-to-peer, not vendor-to-client.
**Lead with:** Their outcome, not our process.
**Ground in:** Their metrics, their competitors, their goals.
**Avoid:** Internal jargon. Use the client's language.

### Website Copy

**Tone:** Warm, clear, confident without being boastful.
**Structure:** Answer-first. What is this page about? Tell them in the first sentence.
**CTA:** Clear, specific, low-pressure. "Book a free 30-minute design call" not "Get started today!"

## Factual Accuracy Rules

### Things AI MUST Get Right

| Topic | Correct Information |
|-------|-------------------|
| Warranty | Lifetime coverage on joints and structural integrity |
| Timeline | 4-8 weeks typical for custom furniture |
| Guarantee | Perfect Fit Guarantee |
| Consultation | Free 30-minute design call |
| Starter package | $2,500-$4,000 for one month |
| Monthly retainer | $3,000-$5,000/month |
| Content client criteria | Professional makers, $200K+ revenue, visually compelling work |
| Founder | Ahmed Hamade |
| Email | info@bespokewoodcraftstudio.com |
| Intake URL | bespokewoodcraftstudio.com/project-intake |

### Things AI Must NOT Fabricate

- Specific pricing for custom furniture projects (varies by project — direct to consultation)
- Client names, case studies, or testimonials not provided in context
- Specific subscriber counts, view counts, or revenue numbers for clients
- Claims about awards, certifications, or press coverage not in context
- Comparisons to specific competitors
- Legal or financial advice
- Health or safety claims about materials (e.g., don't claim a finish is food-safe unless verified)

### When Uncertain

If the AI doesn't have enough context to answer accurately, it should say so and direct to the appropriate resource:
- "I don't have specific pricing for that — the best way to get a clear picture is to book a free design call with Ahmed."
- "I'm not sure about that detail. Email us at info@bespokewoodcraftstudio.com and we'll get back to you."

## Hook Patterns for Social Content

Use these proven patterns when generating social media content:

### 1. First Principles Challenge
Challenge conventional wisdom with evidence from experience.
> "You don't need a jointer to get flat boards. Here's what I do instead."

### 2. Data-Driven Reality Check
Use specific numbers to make abstract problems concrete.
> "A 4/4 white oak board costs $8/bf. A dining table sells for $4,000. The wood is 6% of the price. So where's the other 94% going?"

### 3. Career Arc Story
Connect personal experience to universal lesson.
> "My first commission was a cutting board for $50. I spent 12 hours on it. That's when I learned: pricing isn't about wood."

### 4. Systems Insight
Reveal the hidden system behind a surface problem.
> "Woodworkers don't have a skills problem. They have a marketing problem."

### 5. Vulnerable Admission
Admit a struggle, then share what was learned.
> "I ruined a $400 slab of walnut last month. Rushed the glue-up. The joint failed on day three."

### 6. Shop Floor Lesson
Specific workshop moment that teaches a broader principle.
> "A client asked me to match a 100-year-old oak table. You can't rush patina. Some things only time can build."

### Hooks to AVOID
- "STOP buying [tool] RIGHT NOW" — too aggressive
- "You won't BELIEVE..." — clickbait, disrespects the audience
- "I'm excited to announce..." — too corporate
- "Let me tell you a secret..." — too salesy
- Gear flexing — contradicts "you don't need much" philosophy

## SEO and AI Discoverability Guidelines

### On-Page Best Practices
- Use question-based subheadings that match how people search ("How long does custom furniture take?" not "Timeline Information")
- Answer the question in the first sentence of each section
- Include schema.org structured data (Article, FAQPage, Product, Organization)
- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max) for AI parseability
- Logical H2/H3 hierarchy

### Content Freshness
- Update key pages quarterly at minimum
- Refresh statistics, examples, and case studies
- Add seasonal content where relevant (holiday gifting, spring renovation)

### Cross-Platform Presence
- Maintain consistent messaging across website, social, email, and video
- Build topical authority on: custom furniture, woodworking content, maker marketing, wood species, joinery techniques
- Engage authentically in woodworking communities (Reddit, forums, Facebook groups)

## Content Review Checklist

Before publishing any AI-generated content, verify:

- [ ] **Accuracy:** All facts, prices, timelines, and claims match the ai-context.md file
- [ ] **Voice:** Passes the read-aloud test — sounds like Ahmed talking to a friend
- [ ] **Banned words:** No corporate jargon, filler phrases, or pretentious craft language
- [ ] **Specifics:** Concrete numbers, species, processes — not vague claims
- [ ] **First sentence:** Main point is clear within the first two sentences
- [ ] **Cut test:** 20% of words could be removed without losing meaning
- [ ] **Persona match:** Tone matches the intended audience persona
- [ ] **CTA:** One clear, natural call-to-action (if applicable)
- [ ] **No fabrication:** No invented facts, clients, testimonials, or statistics
- [ ] **Legal safety:** No legal, financial, or health claims outside our expertise
