🔧 Installation Tips for Your Cabinet

✅ All These Are Front-Venting

Every drawer fridge in this list vents from the front grille — meaning you can fully enclose them in a cabinet on the top, sides, and back. No rear clearance needed. This is a major advantage over standard mini fridges, which require 2–3" gaps on all sides.

📏 Cabinet Opening Size

For these 24" units, build your cabinet opening at minimum 24" wide × 23" deep × 34.5" tall. Add an extra 0.5" on each side for easier installation. The front face of the drawers should sit flush with (or slightly recessed from) your cabinet face frame.

🔌 Power Requirements

Standard 115V / 15A household outlet — nothing special needed. Power draw ranges from 87W to 120W across these models. Route the outlet cord through the back of the cabinet. Avoid extension cords for permanent installation — have an outlet inside or directly behind the cabinet.

🔇 Noise Reduction

The cabinet itself dampens 2–4 dB of compressor noise. To go further: add a neoprene anti-vibration mat under the fridge (~$15), and apply peel-and-stick acoustic foam to the inside top panel of the cabinet. Combined, these can bring a 40 dB unit down to ~35 dB perceived.
