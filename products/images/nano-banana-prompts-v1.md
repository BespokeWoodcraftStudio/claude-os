## 5 Essential Tips for Best Results

### 1. **Specific Lighting Beats Generic**
Don't say "good lighting." Instead specify:
- *"Soft directional lighting from upper left at 45-degree angle"*
- *"Warm natural window light, no harsh shadows"*
- *"Studio key light with soft fill light"*

Nano Banana responds to concrete lighting descriptions much better than vague ones.

### 2. **Use Material + Texture Words Together**
Instead of just "matte finish," use:
- *"Matte PLA plastic with slight layer texture typical of 3D printing"*

This helps Nano Banana render the realistic, subtly-layered look of FDM prints without making them look obvious or cheap.

### 3. **Specify Camera Position with Precision**
- *"Overhead flat lay"* vs. *"directly overhead at 90 degrees, centered composition"*
- *"Product sits 12 inches from camera, tilted 15 degrees forward"*
- *"Low angle shot from below, camera at product height"*

Specificity dramatically improves composition consistency.

### 4. **Include Context Elements (Lifestyle) Without Overload**
For lifestyle shots, mention only essential context:
- What surface/environment
- 1-2 complementary objects max
- The lighting/time of day
- Empty space for breathing room

Too many details confuse Nano Banana; it will either include everything chaotically or ignore your product entirely.

### 5. **Color Names + Finish Type**
Use both together:
- *"Sage green matte PLA"* (not just "green")
- *"Ocean blue with warm undertones"* (not just "blue")

This prevents color shifts and ensures the render matches your actual product stock.

## How to Iterate & Refine Prompts

### If the result looks wrong:

**Problem: Color is off**
- Add a reference: *"color similar to Pantone [number]"*
- Specify finish: *"matte, not glossy"*
- Check lighting: warm vs. cool light shifts perceived color

**Problem: Product looks cheap or plasticky**
- Emphasize the texture detail: *"subtle layer lines from FDM printing visible under close inspection"*
- Adjust lighting: softer light makes plastic look better than harsh studio light
- Add context: lifestyle shots look more premium than stark white-background shots

**Problem: Composition is off (too zoomed in, wrong angle)**
- Restate the camera position more explicitly
- Use measurement language: *"product takes up 60% of frame"* or *"product centered in frame with 2 inches padding"*
- Specify depth: *"shallow depth of field with soft background blur"*

**Problem: Background is wrong**
- Be very specific: *"pure white background, seamlessly blended, no visible edge"* (not just "white background")
- For lifestyle: name the specific room type and surface material

**Problem: Missing details or proportions look wrong**
- Request a specific detail prominently: *"close-up shows pen holder cup clearly, sharp focus on opening"*
- Add size context: *"shot in realistic scale context showing product is approximately 6 inches wide"*

### Workflow for iteration:

1. Generate your first prompt as written
2. Review the output image against the product photos in your inventory
3. Document what changed (color, angle, lighting, composition)
4. Modify ONE element in the prompt and regenerate
5. Keep only the best variation for your Etsy upload

## Pro Tips from Successful Nano Banana Creators

1. **Generate in batches.** Create 3 images per prompt, then select the best. Nano Banana's consistency improves with choices.

2. **Test one element at a time.** If a prompt produces a weird result, change only the lighting or angle in your next iteration. This helps you understand what Nano Banana responds to.

3. **Use the same prompt across all colors.** This ensures consistency across your product line. Only change the [COLOR] variable.

4. **Lifestyle shots need breathing room.** Leave 40% of the frame as empty space or soft background. Products look better with context *around* them.

5. **Save failed generations.** Sometimes a "wrong" image reveals a new prompt structure that works better. Keep a folder of iterations.

6. **Request specific material.** "matte PLA" every time. Nano Banana remembers material descriptors and renders them more accurately.

7. **Warm light = premium look.** Cool clinical lighting makes plastic look cheap. Warm 3000-4000K light makes organizers look luxurious.
