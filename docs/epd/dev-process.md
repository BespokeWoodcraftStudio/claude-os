## Overview

We run a monthly production cycle per client, anchored by an editorial calendar. Each month starts with planning and ends with a retrospective. Within the month, individual content pieces flow through a linear pipeline: brief, shoot, edit, review, publish. The goal is predictable output with room for creative flexibility.

---

## Methodology

### Monthly Production Cycles

Each client operates on a monthly cycle. Content is planned, produced, and published within a rolling 30-day window. Batch shooting keeps production efficient — we aim to capture a full month's video content in 1-2 shoot days.

**Cycle structure:**

| Phase | Duration | What Happens |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| Planning & Briefing | Days 1-5 | Review last month's analytics, set content themes, finalize editorial calendar with client |
| Production (Shoot + Edit) | Days 5-22 | Batch film content, edit videos, create social assets, draft copy |
| Review & Publish | Days 22-30 | Client reviews via Frame.io, revisions, scheduling and publishing across platforms |

---

## How Work Flows

```
Content Brief → Shoot Day → Rough Cut → Client Review → Final Edit → Publish → Measure
```

### Planning & Briefing

At the start of each month, the team reviews the previous month's performance data (views, engagement, leads generated) with the client. Based on what's working, we set themes and topics for the upcoming month and lock the editorial calendar. The client approves the calendar before any production begins.

### Production

Shoot days are batched — typically 1-2 days on-site in the client's shop. The team captures enough footage for a full month of content (long-form videos, short clips, B-roll, photos). Editing begins within 48 hours of a shoot. Rough cuts go to the client within 5 business days.

### Review & Publishing

Clients review content on Frame.io with timestamped comments. We allow up to two rounds of revisions per piece. Once approved, content is scheduled and published using native platform tools (YouTube Studio, Meta Business Suite, etc.). Post-publish, we track performance and feed insights back into next month's planning.

## Quality Standards

Content quality is the product. Every piece goes through these checkpoints before publishing:

- **Peer review:** Every edit is reviewed by at least one other team member before going to the client
- **Client approval:** Nothing publishes without explicit client sign-off on Frame.io
- **Brand consistency check:** Final content is checked against the client's brand guide (colors, tone, logo usage)

---

## AI-Assisted Production

We use AI tools to handle repetitive production tasks so the team stays focused on creative work. Specifically:

- **Transcription and captions:** Auto-generated, then reviewed and corrected by a team member
- **Social copy drafts:** AI generates first drafts of captions and descriptions; a human edits for voice and accuracy
- **Content repurposing:** AI tools suggest clip selections from long-form video; the editor makes final cut decisions

Human judgment is required for all creative decisions, client-facing copy, and final approvals. AI is a tool in the workflow, not a replacement for the team.
