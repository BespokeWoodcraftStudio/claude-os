At Bespoke Woodcraft Studio, our Culture of Documentation is a foundational aspect of how we work. As a distributed, asynchronous team, our ability to thrive relies on clear, comprehensive, and well-organized knowledge that is accessible to everyone.

---

## Why Documentation is Crucial

- **Efficiency**: Good documentation helps us solve problems without waiting for synchronous interaction.
- **Freedom**: Team members work in different time zones. Documentation eliminates blockers.
- **Scalability**: This is how we function effectively as a remote organization.

---

## What Does It Mean to Have a Culture of Documentation?

We collectively work towards:
- Ensuring our knowledge base is useful, tidy, and up to date
- Fixing errors whenever they arise
- Adding missing information as soon as we identify the gap
- Enabling others to learn and build upon our work

---

## Single Source of Truth (SSoT)

Our SSoT is this Context OS knowledge base (stored in Git) for company knowledge, processes, and brand guidelines. For day-to-day project work, we use Notion as our shared workspace for content calendars, client boards, and task tracking. Git is the canonical source for anything about how we operate; Notion is where live project work happens. If something conflicts, the Context OS docs win.

---

## Who Owns Documentation?

- **Page Owners**: Every document has an assigned owner responsible for keeping it accurate
- **Department Ownership**: Documentation is owned by the department or subject matter experts where it originates
- **Contribution**: If you notice something that needs improvement, either fix it or let the owner know

---

## Best Practices

- **Be Clear and Concise**: Make documentation easily understood by anyone reading it
- **Add Value**: Only document what is valuable
- **Keep It Updated**: Stale information can be misleading
- **Organize Wisely**: Structure information logically and link related documents
- **Include Dates**: Add timestamps so everyone knows how recent the information is

---

## Our Documentation Mantra

"If it's worth knowing, it's worth writing down."
