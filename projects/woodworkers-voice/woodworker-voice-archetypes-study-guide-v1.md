## How to Use This Guide

This study guide serves three purposes:

1. **Voice reference** — When writing a how-to article, scan the archetype section to choose the right voice for your audience and topic.
2. **Creator study** — Watch or read the listed creators to internalize their voice. Steal patterns, not words.
3. **Blend guide** — Use the recommended archetype blend at the end to maintain Bespoke Woodcraft Studio's voice consistency.

## The Top 20: Profiles, Quotes & Archetypes

### 1. Ishitani Furniture (Natsuki Ishitani)

**Platform:** YouTube | **Archetype: The Silent Master**

Japanese master furniture maker whose videos contain no narration — just the sound of tools meeting wood, filmed by his wife Chie. Mixes traditional Japanese techniques with modern equipment. His work communicates entirely through demonstrated mastery.

> *Silence is the voice. The hand plane speaks. The dovetail speaks. The finished piece speaks loudest of all.*

**Style markers:** No narration, ASMR-quality workshop sound, construction-documentary format, traditional-meets-modern technique.

**Steal this for:** Photo-heavy tutorials, visual step-by-step guides, Instagram content where the images carry the instruction.

---

### 2. Paul Sellers

**Platform:** YouTube, Blog | **Followers:** ~689K | **Archetype: The Patient Master**

The global authority on hand tool woodworking. British warmth meets decades of workshop wisdom. His content is methodical, patient, deeply educational — focused on beginners without ever being condescending. Pioneered the hand tool resurgence.

> *"I'm not trying to make woodworking look easy. I'm trying to make it look possible."*

**Style markers:** Unhurried pacing, precise language, "let me show you" cadence, respects the learner's intelligence, never skips a step.

**Steal this for:** Beginner fundamentals, hand tool technique articles, any content where patience and precision matter more than speed.

---

### 3. Blacktail Studio (Cam Anderson)

**Platform:** YouTube, Instagram | **Followers:** ~3.4M | **Archetype: The Narrative Craftsman**

Former emergency transport pilot turned full-time maker. Creates high-production content around live-edge and epoxy work. Selects projects based on their story potential, not just profit margin. Films on iPhone, proving vision matters more than gear.

> *"I don't choose projects based on what will sell. I choose them based on what will make a good story."*

**Style markers:** Story arc in every build, cinematic pacing, context before technique, "here's why this piece matters" framing.

**Steal this for:** Project features, client work stories, articles where the motivation behind the build is as interesting as the build itself.

---

### 4. Steve Ramsey (Woodworking for Mere Mortals)

**Platform:** YouTube | **Followers:** ~1.89M | **Archetype: The Friendly Democratizer**

Makes woodworking accessible to complete beginners. Charismatic, warm, self-deprecating humor. Focuses on projects people can actually build without big shops or expensive tools. Reframes limitations as possibilities.

> *"You don't need a $3,000 table saw. You need a $40 circular saw and the confidence to use it."*

**Style markers:** "You can do this" energy, minimal tool requirements, humor as confidence-builder, celebrates simplicity.

**Steal this for:** Beginner how-tos, budget builds, "first project" articles, content aimed at people who've never touched a saw.

---

### 5. Essential Craftsman (Scott Wadsworth)

**Platform:** YouTube | **Followers:** ~1.25M | **Archetype: The Philosophical Tradesman**

40+ years of trade experience in blacksmithing, carpentry, and masonry. Content transcends instruction — it's about the dignity and meaning of manual work. His voice carries the weight of a lifetime in the trades.

> *"Everything civilization required emerged between the face of an anvil and the face of a hammer."*

**Style markers:** Moral weight, historical context, trades as calling, "this matters" undercurrent, no rush, no fluff.

**Steal this for:** Opinion pieces, craft philosophy, "why woodworking" articles, content that needs to feel significant.

---

### 6. Bourbon Moth Woodworking (Jason Hibbs)

**Platform:** YouTube, Instagram | **Followers:** ~1.24M | **Archetype: The Honest Maker**

Started in an Oregon garage. 90% of builds come from audience suggestions. Explicitly rejects algorithmic pressure — films on iPhone, prioritizes "aesthetic sincerity" over production polish. Humor-driven, self-deprecating, real.

> *"I'm not a master woodworker. I'm a guy who makes stuff and sometimes it works out."*

**Style markers:** Self-deprecating humor, transparent about failures, audience-driven topics, authenticity over perfection.

**Steal this for:** Relatable how-tos, "things I learned the hard way" articles, mistake-prevention content, anything where vulnerability builds trust.

---

### 7. Pask Makes (Neil Paskin)

**Platform:** YouTube, Instagram | **Followers:** ~1.2M | **Archetype: The Poetic Explorer**

Australian maker who uses stop-motion animation to narrate builds. Range includes Kumiko, wooden fans, kayaks. Each video is a film, not a tutorial. Models "learning in public" with intellectual humility.

> *"I don't claim to be an expert. I'm learning through doing, and I want to take you along."*

**Style markers:** Wonder and curiosity, learning-in-public, poetic minimalism, admits not-knowing, treats each build as discovery.

**Steal this for:** "I tried this new technique" articles, journey-format content, exploratory builds, content that makes uncertainty feel exciting.

---

### 8. Foureyes Furniture (Chris Salomone)

**Platform:** YouTube, Instagram | **Followers:** ~1.4M | **Archetype: The Design Storyteller**

Furniture designer-engineer making sleek, modern, architecturally precise pieces. Pioneered the "narrative filmmaking" approach to woodworking content — macro shots, environmental wides, improvised guitar. Treats the viewer as aesthetically intelligent.

> *"I wanted the videos to feel like something, not just teach something."*

**Style markers:** Design thinking, emotional resonance in instruction, respects reader's visual intelligence, modern/minimal aesthetic.

**Steal this for:** Design-forward project articles, modern furniture features, content where aesthetics and technique are equally important.

---

### 9. Samurai Carpenter (Jesse de Geest)

**Platform:** YouTube, Instagram | **Followers:** ~858K | **Archetype: The Bridge Builder**

Canadian woodworker blending traditional Japanese carpentry with modern tools. Personality-driven, humorous, deeply technical on pull saws, wooden joinery, timber framing. Makes centuries-old wisdom feel fresh.

> *"The old ways aren't old because they stopped working. They're old because they've been working for centuries."*

**Style markers:** Cross-cultural technique, humor + depth, honors tradition while being accessible, "there's wisdom here" framing.

**Steal this for:** Technique comparison articles, cultural/historical context pieces, joinery deep-dives, content bridging old and new methods.

---

### 10. Marc Spagnuolo (The Wood Whisperer)

**Platform:** YouTube, Instagram | **Followers:** ~850K + 300K | **Archetype: The Professional Mentor**

First online woodworking video series (started 2006). Fine furniture focus with instruction at all skill levels. Professional, accessible, dry humor. Built a sustainable education business without sacrificing quality.

> *"Good woodworking isn't about perfection. It's about understanding what the wood wants to do and working with it."*

**Style markers:** Instructional clarity, professional warmth, never shows off, treats every skill level with respect, dry wit.

**Steal this for:** Technique instruction at any level, tool reviews, content that needs to feel authoritative without feeling academic.

### 11. Jimmy Diresta

**Platform:** YouTube | **Followers:** ~2.02M | **Archetype: The Cinematic Maker**

Zen-like presentations with Hollywood-level production. Slow-motion, time-lapses, creative camera work across woodworking and metalworking. Treats workshop footage like cinematic art.

> *"Just make."*

**Style markers:** Production as respect, visual beauty in process, "less talk, more craft" ethos, meditative pacing.

**Steal this for:** Visually-led content, workshop photography direction, articles where the making process is the spectacle.

### 12. Rex Krueger

**Platform:** YouTube, Blog | **Followers:** ~500K+ | **Archetype: The Accessible Scholar**

Former university professor (Writing and Media Studies) who left academia for woodworking. Teaches low-cost craft using hand tools and pre-Industrial techniques. Intellectual rigor meets radical accessibility.

> *"You don't need money to make beautiful things. You need knowledge, a few good tools, and the patience to learn."*

**Style markers:** Academic clarity in plain language, budget-first philosophy, historical context, treats craft knowledge as democratized.

**Steal this for:** Budget how-tos, hand tool guides, "woodworking on any income" articles, content needing intellectual weight without jargon.

### 13. Anne Briggs (Anne of All Trades)

**Platform:** YouTube, Instagram | **Followers:** ~650K+ | **Archetype: The Whole-Life Maker**

Self-taught maker, farmer, carpenter, blacksmith, welder. Nashville-based. Integrates craft with sustainable homesteading. No-BS tutorials. Making as a way of engaging with the world, not monetizing an audience.

> *"I don't make things because I'm trying to build an audience. I make things because they need to be made."*

**Style markers:** Matter-of-fact competence, life-integrated craft, no performance, grounding authenticity, multi-disciplinary.

**Steal this for:** Homesteading-adjacent content, articles connecting woodworking to broader living, content needing grounded, no-nonsense authority.

### 14. April Wilkerson

**Platform:** YouTube, Instagram | **Followers:** ~1.59M | **Archetype: The Self-Made Pioneer**

Self-taught from budget home renovation. Pioneering female voice in woodworking. Transparent about learning in real time. Story is about independence and resourcefulness as much as finished pieces.

> *"I didn't start because I knew what I was doing. I started because something needed to be built."*

**Style markers:** "If I can figure this out, so can you" energy, learning documented in real time, empowerment through making.

**Steal this for:** Empowerment-oriented how-tos, content for people starting from zero, articles where the journey matters as much as the destination.

### 15. Michael Alm (AlmFab)

**Platform:** YouTube, Instagram | **Followers:** ~500K+ | **Archetype: The Experimental Artist**

Seattle designer with sculpture background. Known for patterned plywood experiments and "melting tables." Called "The Chocolatier of Woodworking." Art-first approach to functional furniture.

> *"What happens if you laminate 47 layers of dyed plywood and then turn it on a lathe? Let's find out."*

**Style markers:** "What if?" curiosity, fine art sensibility, celebrates experimentation, design innovation as instruction.

**Steal this for:** Advanced technique articles, design challenge content, "pushing the boundaries" features, creative inspiration pieces.

### 16. Laura Kampf

**Platform:** YouTube, Instagram | **Followers:** ~846K | **Archetype: The European Polymath**

German maker/designer across woodworking, metalworking, and inventive crafting. European design sensibility — clean lines, functional beauty, minimal waste. Confident without being loud.

> *"I don't separate making from thinking. The best ideas come when my hands are already moving."*

**Style markers:** Design-first, multi-disciplinary, international perspective, precision and intentionality, thinking-through-making.

**Steal this for:** Design-forward content, cross-disciplinary projects, articles needing a sophisticated international perspective.

### 17. James Wright (Wood By Wright)

**Platform:** YouTube, Podcast | **Followers:** ~200K+ | **Archetype: The Principled Purist**

30+ years experience. Pure hand tools, no sponsorships, complete editorial independence. Focused on "the joy of woodworking rather than just the method."

> *"Hand tools teach you to listen to the wood. Power tools teach you to listen to yourself. Both matter, but one is quieter."*

**Style markers:** Contemplative depth, constraint as philosophy, editorial independence, joy over efficiency.

**Steal this for:** Hand tool guides, slow craft content, mindfulness-meets-making articles, content that needs philosophical gravity.

**Platform:** YouTube, Newsletter | **Followers:** ~1M | **Archetype: The Methodical Educator**

One of the longest-running woodworking channels (since 2011). 100% woodworking-focused. Clear narration, even pacing, professional production. 14 years of consistent, reliable instruction.

> *"The most expensive plane is nothing but a paperweight unless properly sharpened."*

**Style markers:** No wasted words, methodical structure, professional consistency, practical wisdom, tool-mastery focus.

**Steal this for:** Tool guides, sharpening articles, systematic skill-building content, anything needing textbook-quality clarity.

**Platform:** YouTube, TikTok | **Followers:** ~810K + 577K | **Archetype: The Resourceful Innovator**

Canadian maker building furniture from 3D-printed parts and reclaimed pallets. Transforms waste into high-design pieces. Videos regularly hit 4-8M views. Landed a TV show.

**Style markers:** Waste-to-design philosophy, resourcefulness as creativity, accessible materials, design innovation from constraints.

### 20. Nick Offerman

**Platform:** Books, Podcasts, Speaking, Social Media | **Followers:** 1.8M+ (Instagram), significant book/podcast reach | **Archetype: The Philosopher-Advocate**

Actor, author (Good Clean Fun, Paddle Your Own Canoe), and lifelong woodworker who frames craft as moral and aesthetic practice. Runs Offerman Woodshop in Los Angeles — a real production shop, not a celebrity vanity project. His influence on woodworking culture is massive despite not being a traditional YouTuber. Advocates for making as antidote to consumerism, gateway to community, and practice of patience and humility. Blends dry deadpan humor with genuine philosophical depth.

> *"Making things with hands, tools, and wood is for everybody."*

> *"The things I've made with my own two hands have been the most satisfying purchases of my life — except they weren't purchases at all."*

**Style markers:** Philosophical weight with deadpan humor, craft as worldview, inclusive ethos, celebrates the human need to make, draws connections between woodworking and character, anti-consumerist without being preachy.

**Steal this for:** Brand manifesto content, "why we do this" articles, philosophical framing, content needing cultural significance, introductions to woodworking for non-woodworkers.

## Applying Archetypes to How-To Articles

### Step 1: Choose your archetype blend

Every how-to article should have a primary voice (60-70%) and a secondary voice (20-30%), with an optional accent (10%). The blend depends on your audience and topic.

**Example blends:**

| Article type | Primary | Secondary | Accent |
|-------------|---------|-----------|--------|
| Beginner dovetail guide | Patient Master | Friendly Democratizer | Honest Maker |
| Live-edge table feature | Narrative Craftsman | Philosophical Tradesman | — |
| Budget workbench build | Friendly Democratizer | Honest Maker | Resourceful Innovator |
| Hand plane buyer's guide | Methodical Educator | Patient Master | Principled Purist |
| "Why I quit my job to build furniture" | Philosophical Tradesman | Honest Maker | Poetic Explorer |
| Japanese joinery technique | Bridge Builder | Patient Master | Silent Master |

### Step 2: Set your voice dials

Five elements to calibrate for each article:

| Dial | Low setting | High setting |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| **Authority** | "Here's one way to do it" | "Set your marking gauge to exactly 3/16 inch" |
| **Warmth** | Factual, straightforward | "This part looks scary. It's actually the easiest step." |
| **Clarity** | Narrative, flowing | "Clamp. Mark. Cut to the waste side." |
| **Humor** | Serious, focused | "Ask me how I know. Actually, don't." |
| **Philosophy** | Pure technique | "Dovetails aren't just strong. They're a conversation between two pieces of wood." |

### Step 3: Write a test paragraph

Before writing the full article, draft one paragraph in your chosen voice. Read it aloud. Does it sound like the archetype? Adjust until it does.

## Recommended Blend for Bespoke Woodcraft Studio

Based on the studio's existing voice direction — *"Warm, calm, and clear. Like a craftsman walking you through how something works — no rush, no jargon, just genuine knowledge shared generously."*

**Primary (60%): The Patient Master**
Deep knowledge shared without ego. Precise, warm, unhurried. "Here's what I've learned. Let me walk you through it."

**Secondary (25%): The Honest Maker**
Transparent about challenges, uses light humor, builds trust through imperfection. "I messed this up the first time. Here's the fix."

**Accent (15%): The Philosophical Tradesman**
Connects technique to meaning. Reminds readers why craft matters. "There's something about a hand-cut joint that a machine can't replicate."

**The resulting voice:** A skilled friend who knows their stuff, shares freely, admits mistakes, and occasionally reminds you why this craft matters.

## Study Plan

To internalize these voices, watch or read one creator per week:

**Week 1-4 (Core voices):** Paul Sellers, Steve Ramsey, Bourbon Moth, Essential Craftsman
**Week 5-8 (Elevation voices):** Blacktail Studio, Pask Makes, Chris Salomone, Samurai Carpenter
**Week 9-12 (Specialist voices):** Ishitani, Rex Krueger, Wood By Wright, Laura Kampf

While watching, note: How do they open? How do they handle mistakes? What's their sentence rhythm? How do they transition from "why" to "how"?
