This document serves as a guide to how we communicate at Bespoke Woodcraft Studio. Since we are a distributed team, we rely on both sync and async, and each serves a different role.

---

## Definitions

- **Sync Communication**: Communication happening in real-time, like meetings, DMs, huddles, or calls. Best for immediate, real-time problem-solving.
- **Async Communication**: Communication that doesn't require an immediate response. Ideal for deep thinking, announcements, or requests that a person needs to see.
- **Async by Default**: A mindset that prefers async methods first. Ask yourself: "Does this need to be a meeting?" If it can be handled async, choose that route.

---

## Benefits of Async by Default

1. **Deep, Focused Work**: Fewer interruptions mean more opportunities to focus and achieve flow.
2. **Faster Delivery**: Async allows people to contribute according to their schedules, optimizing workflow around the clock.
3. **Flexibility and Lower Stress**: Team members have control over their own schedules.
4. **Inclusion**: Async ensures work isn't bound by time zones.
5. **Knowledge Retention**: Async encourages thorough documentation, making knowledge more accessible.

---

## How We Communicate

### We Assume Good Intent

Assuming good intent means believing that your colleagues are not trying to harm or ignore you. Instead of assuming the worst from a short message, interpret it positively.

### We Self-Serve Before Reaching Out

Before asking for help, look through existing resources such as the knowledge base or previous discussions. Self-serving helps reduce noise and ensures we don't repeat questions.

### We Default to Public Communication

We communicate in public channels whenever possible to maintain transparency, avoid silos, and encourage collaboration.

**When to Use Private Channels or DMs:**
- Sensitive or confidential discussions
- Personal or HR-related conversations

### We Keep Conversations Close to the Work

Reply in the same place where the discussion started. Use threads or comments to keep context intact.

### We Set Expectations for Responses

- **Timely Acknowledgment**: Use reactions to indicate you have seen a message.
- **Response Expectations**: Generally, reply within one working day unless marked as urgent.

---

## When to Use Sync Communication

Some situations call for synchronous communication:
- **1:1 Meetings**: For personal topics or feedback
- **Project Kickoffs**: To align expectations quickly
- **Social Interaction**: Virtual coffee chats or onboarding
- **Urgent issues**: Something is on fire, or you need immediate help

---

## Avoid Sync for These Scenarios

- **Status Updates**: Use written posts or updates
- **Simple Decisions**: Use messages or comments
- **Report Creation**: Use written formats shared asynchronously

---

## What Async Looks Like in Practice

- **Public Communication**: Share non-sensitive information in open channels
- **Document Everything**: Project decisions, status updates, and FAQs should be documented
- **Async Meetings**: Prepare a document and invite others to collaborate asynchronously before scheduling a meeting
- **Over-Communicate**: Be proactive and share updates frequently
- **Default to Action**: Execute tasks even if all details aren't perfect, and adapt as needed
- **Recording and Agendas**: Always have an agenda for recurring meetings and record them for those unable to attend
