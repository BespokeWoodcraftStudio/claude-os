Bespoke Woodcraft Studio exists to help makers share their craft with the world. We believe the best content comes from people who genuinely care about the work — and that means building a team culture rooted in the same values our clients live by: patience, honesty, and pride in what you make.

The main goal for our culture is straightforward:
- To do work we're proud to put our name on.
- To create an environment where curiosity and generosity come naturally.
- To help every maker we work with feel seen, heard, and understood.

---

## Guiding Values

**Craftsmanship in Everything:** We treat every piece of content the way a woodworker treats a joint — with care, intention, and a refusal to cut corners.

**Generosity in Teaching:** We share what we know freely. Holding back knowledge doesn't protect us; it just makes the community smaller.

**Honesty Over Polish:** Real resonates. We'd rather show a genuine workshop with sawdust on the floor than a staged photo that feels hollow.

**Show the Work:** Process matters as much as the finished piece. We document the journey — the failures, the breakthroughs, the learning.

---

## Guiding Principles

### Craftsmanship in Everything

Every thumbnail, script, blog post, and social caption represents both us and the maker we're working with. We take the time to get the details right — the pacing of an edit, the clarity of a headline, the accuracy of a technique description. Rushing is the enemy of good work.

**We Don't:**
- Ship content we haven't reviewed carefully
- Use generic templates when a client deserves something tailored
- Treat any deliverable as "just a quick one"

**We Do:**
- Review our own work before asking someone else to review it
- Learn enough about each client's craft to represent it accurately
- Take pride in the small details that most people won't consciously notice but will feel

---

### Generosity in Teaching

Our clients are makers, not marketers. Part of our job is helping them understand why something works, not just handing them a finished product. We also share knowledge with each other constantly — when one person learns a new editing technique or discovers a platform change, everyone benefits.

**We Don't:**
- Gatekeep knowledge to feel indispensable
- Make clients feel dumb for not knowing marketing terminology
- Hoard processes or shortcuts

**We Do:**
- Explain our reasoning and teach clients along the way
- Share tips, resources, and discoveries in our team channels
- Create internal guides when we figure out something worth repeating

---

### Honesty Over Polish

The woodworking community values authenticity. They can spot a fake from across the shop. Our content needs to reflect real people doing real work — imperfections included. This applies internally too: we speak plainly about what's working and what isn't.

**We Don't:**
- Over-produce content until it loses its soul
- Promise results we can't deliver
- Avoid hard conversations about quality or direction

**We Do:**
- Let the maker's real personality come through in every piece
- Give honest feedback to clients, even when it's uncomfortable
- Acknowledge mistakes quickly and fix them without drama

---

### Show the Work

The best content in the maker space comes from process, not just finished pieces. We apply this same principle to how we work: we document our thinking, share works in progress, and keep our process visible so clients and teammates always know where things stand.

**We Don't:**
- Disappear into a black box and emerge with a finished product
- Skip documenting decisions or rationale
- Hide behind vague status updates

**We Do:**
- Share drafts and rough cuts early for feedback
- Keep clients in the loop at every stage of production
- Write down what we tried, what worked, and what we'd do differently
